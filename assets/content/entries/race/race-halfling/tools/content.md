# Halfling

## Character creation

+3 health

Movement: 4 / 3

Cannot use large weapons and shields

## Advancement

+3 health

## Features

### Unseen: Heroic action

Make an attack with advantage.

### Dodge and weave: Heroic action

Make a major move without drawing an opportunity attack.

### Hamstring: Heroic action

Make an agility vs. agility attack against a creature that does damage equal to your strength that cannot be ignored. If the attack hits, that creature cannot take major actions until the beginning of the next round.

### Woodland Roots: Feature

Acquire 2 nature spells and gain 2 mana. Lose 1 agility.

### Woodland Power: Feature

Gain 3 mana.

### Nimble Escape: Feature

You may move through creatures that are larger than you.

### Halfling Luck: Heroic Reaction

When you fail an attack roll, defense roll, or expertise check, you may roll again and gain advantage to the new roll.

### Master cook: Feature

Spend 3 coins to purchase from a market and provide a meal for the entire party. Double the healing everyone receives from resting that day.

## About Halflings

Halflings do not keep records of their history, so what is known about them is largely because of the humans who have welcomed them into communities and kingdoms. Halflings seem to be everywhere, with consistent habits and customs. This includes their propensity to till the land, dwell in peace with civilized races, provide wares and services in markets, and operate inns as havens for travelers.

Wherever there are human villages and cities, halflings commonly build smaller settlements nearby. This is a way to live within the protection of humans, who offer safety in exchange for the benefits halflings provide to human communities. This includes sustenance, local knowledge of the land, and artisanal items and equipment.

Some halflings feel the call to adventure, finding joy in exploring the natural beauty of wild lands. When faced with combat, they are elusive and often take advantage of stealth, surprise, and exploiting weaknesses.

Other halflings develop a deep connection to the land that they dwell in, allowing them to draw upon its natural powers as a font of magic. These halflings are often the community sage, providing wisdom and experience to younger members.

As they increase their connection to the powers of nature, they can become more withdrawn from society. These halflings become an important source of knowledge for anyone seeking to understand the land and its animal inhabitants. However, this knowledge is not given easily and often must be earned by caring for and protecting the land, including the animals and people that dwell on it.
