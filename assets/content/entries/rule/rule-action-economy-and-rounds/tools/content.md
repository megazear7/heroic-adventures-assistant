# Rule: Action Economy and Rounds

## Scope

Use this rule for complete turn-timing adjudication in combat:
- card-flip activation timing
- what action types are legal at each timing window
- per-round/per-card limits
- conflicts between reactions, opportunity actions, and bonus actions

## Consolidated Rule

Combat runs on a 10-card encounter deck per level band. Each card flip opens an activation window for creatures in the shown initiative range (or all creatures on the all-players/all-monsters cards). During legal activation, creatures can use action types as follows:

- **Major action**: core turn action; most creatures get at most one major action per round.
- **Minor action**: utility/mobility/support actions; available when timing and action choice allow.
- **Heroic action**: special high-impact actions gated by heroic action uses per day, unless overridden by effects (for example bravery potions).
- **Bonus action**: available when the currently flipped range is higher than the previous range, or when a range card is first flipped in round 1 of that round.
- **Reaction**: triggered by feature text; limited to one reaction per initiative card.
- **Opportunity action**: triggered primarily by enemy major movement leaving melee threat.

## Round Procedure

1. Build and shuffle encounter deck.
2. Flip one card at a time.
3. Activate matching creatures; they take legal actions for that window.
4. Resolve triggered reactions/opportunity actions immediately when triggers occur.
5. Continue until all 10 cards resolve.
6. End round, then reshuffle and start next round if combat continues.

## Key Interactions

- Surprise causes affected creatures to skip their first initiative card in round 1.
- Heroic actions generally permit major movement and consume heroic resources.
- A creature can only use one reaction per initiative card, even if multiple triggers occur.
- Opportunity actions depend on threatened positioning and major movement, not minor movement.
- Bonus action availability is card-order dependent, not creature-choice dependent.

## Adjudication Notes

- Resolve action legality first, then dice/mechanical outcomes.
- If multiple triggers fire simultaneously, resolve in initiative-owner order unless a specific feature states otherwise.
- When uncertain, prefer the more restrictive timing interpretation and communicate clearly to players.
