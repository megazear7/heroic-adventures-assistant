# Rule: Movement, Positioning, and Opportunity

## Scope

Use this rule for board movement, flanking, threat zones, opportunity triggers, and corner/edge movement legality.

## Consolidated Rule

Movement uses square-grid counting where edge-to-edge and corner-to-corner each cost 1 space. Major movement generally risks opportunity actions; minor movement does not.

## Threat and Opportunity

- A creature is **threatened** when in melee range of an enemy.
- Leaving threatened melee range with major movement can trigger opportunity action.
- Opportunity trigger requires movement to a space that is at least as far or farther from the threatening creature.
- Moving only one space with major movement does not draw opportunity actions.

## Positioning Rules

- Flanking grants advantage when allies are exactly opposite around target.
- Tight diagonal movement between blocked corners/enemy-adjacent constraints can trigger opportunity exposure.
- Ranged attacks made while adjacent to enemies can provoke opportunity attacks.

## Standard Movement Actions

- Minor Move: minor action, no opportunity draw.
- Major Move: major action, opportunity draw as normal.
- Attack and Bonus Attack can include split movement before/after attack.

## Adjudication Notes

- Confirm start square, path, and destination before resolving triggers.
- Resolve opportunity action immediately at trigger point.
- If path legality is ambiguous, use strict line/adjacency interpretation and keep consistency across session.
