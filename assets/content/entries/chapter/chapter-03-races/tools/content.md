## About Humans

The humans of Heroic Adventures are explorers, builders, and warriors. Some expand into lands unknown and live in remote villages. These folk push the boundaries of civilization. They often cultivate the earth and subdue it to their basic needs of food and shelter. Others discover ancient secrets that might be left behind by the elder races such as elves and dwarves. They incorporate the knowledge of anyone they encounter and bend it to their needs. Their mighty cities with towers reaching to the skies are imposing even to the most aggressive of foes.

In all of their endeavors, humans tend to come into conflict with others. More than this, there are much more terrifying forces at work than the mere misaligned goals of the civilized. Orcs might always be amassing over the next mountain range or beyond the next forest. Goblins are ever on the move and harassing the edges of civilization. More than this, forces of darkness are always seeking to corrupt, gain power, and unleash destruction at the most opportune time.

## About Elves

The Elves of Heroic Adventurers have a rich, often tragic history. Long ago, they built vast empires across the world's continents, only to see them fall due to calamities unleashed by their ancient foe, Zeerzashra: the Spider Queen. Some elves, the High Elves, retreated to remote regions, preserving powerful magic untouched by the War of the Dead. Rare, war-hardened, and aloof, they judge others harshly.

Others, descended from the Betrayers or in conflict with Elven Lords, left their lands. These Wood Elves became nomadic forest spirits, mastering wilderness skills. Over time, some wanderers formed small communities, becoming Grey Elves — neither noble nor wild, but akin to humans, who prize their magic and ancient wisdom. Grey Elves have even surpassed the magical prowess of their High Elven kin. In some regions, they’ve intermarried with humans and becoming half elves, forging bonds that bring either peace or strife, depending on the locale.

## About Dwarves

The Dwarves of Heroic Adventures are diverse, encompassing warriors and conquerors seldom finding solace with human societies. Their lifestyle and customs are largely unknown to outsiders. Other dwarves are natural allies of humans, confronting akin enemies and dangers. These dwarves often intermingle with human society, maintaining distinct political hierarchies, familial residences, and social practices. Dwarves build subterranean cities beneath human cities, just as capacious and intricate, if not more so. Due to the rarity of dwarves siring offspring, they often sell habitations to gnomes, halflings, and humans.

Dwarves are robust and stalwart; once their mind is determined, it is difficult to dissuade them from their chosen course of action. They are also resistant to magic, a skill cultivated in days of yore after protracted conflicts with elves. In those ancient times, they forged counter-magic based on runes and symbols, a lore any dwarf can acquire with assiduous training.

## About Halflings

Halflings do not keep records of their history, so what is known about them is largely because of the humans who have welcomed them into communities and kingdoms. Halflings seem to be everywhere, with consistent habits and customs. This includes their propensity to till the land, dwell in peace with civilized races, provide wares and services in markets, and operate inns as havens for travelers.

Wherever there are human villages and cities, halflings commonly build smaller settlements nearby. This is a way to live within the protection of humans, who offer safety in exchange for the benefits halflings provide to human communities. This includes sustenance, local knowledge of the land, and artisanal items and equipment.

Some halflings feel the call to adventure, finding joy in exploring the natural beauty of wild lands. When faced with combat, they are elusive and often take advantage of stealth, surprise, and exploiting weaknesses.

Other halflings develop a deep connection to the land that they dwell in, allowing them to draw upon its natural powers as a font of magic. These halflings are often the community sage, providing wisdom and experience to younger members.

As they increase their connection to the powers of nature, they can become more withdrawn from society. These halflings become an important source of knowledge for anyone seeking to understand the land and its animal inhabitants. However, this knowledge is not given easily and often must be earned by caring for and protecting the land, including the animals and people that dwell on it.

## About Halflings

The gnomes of Heroic Adventures have lived side by side with the dwarves for time immemorial. Being the lesser race in terms of both stature and number, the gnomes rarely have political clout within the community. However, they have skills with metal, producing equipment that exceeds the craft of any other race and intricate trinkets with complexity that baffles even the greatest human engineers.

Gnomes can be furious in combat. While not as strong as a dwarf they well exceed the strength of halflings, who in appearance they are most similar to. Gnomes, as often as dwarves or humans, can engage in warfare. However, like everything else, gnomes are almost never found acting alone but almost always following the leadership of dwarves.

## About Half Orcs

In Heroic Adventures, war with orcs is as old a story as there is. Orcs can be found beyond the border of every civilized land. In these border areas, half orcs are common. While their origins are up for debate, they are now an established part of human society. They enjoy manual labor, are naturally skilled in warfare, and almost always have a dour attitude. While not being numerous enough or having the necessary instincts for political organization, they instead are mostly recruited by humans and dwarves.

Classes

In *Heroic Adventures*, every player character will have a character class. Each character class will give you certain benefits during character creation and level advancement. The most significant of these benefits are features, which you will select at certain levels as described in the level advancement section.

There are 4 basic classes: fighter, wizard, rogue, and cleric. These are the standard roles that a character can take when adventuring through a fantastical world.

The *fighter* is someone who takes up weapons and armor and leads his allies into the unknown. A fighter will take on challenges that others might back away from and is used to being in the chaos of combat. It is recommended that players new to tabletop roleplaying play one of the basic classes.

The *wizard* is someone who casts spells to overcome enemies. Often wizards are not strong in combat, although over time they can gain the ability to be a combat wizard or can specialize in powerful spellcasting.

The *rogue* likes to stay out of the way of the enemy and take advantage of their weaknesses.

The *cleric* is a warrior who can also cast spells, mostly focused on healing and helping their friends. Clerics are also capable of powerfully confronting undead creatures.

There are also advanced classes. These classes have more complicated abilities and usually change the standard rules of play in some way. It is recommended to only play the advanced classes if you are an experienced tabletop role player.

The *priest* is devoted to a religious order and uses divine intervention to aid his companions in combat.

The *paladin* is a divine warrior who, like the fighter, wants to be in the midst of combat. A paladin is unafraid of combat and even death itself.

The *warlock* is a ruthless spell caster who uses his own lifeblood to cast devastating incantations.

The *barbarian* is a warrior who focuses on aggression and power to overcome his enemies.

The *druid* uses the forces of nature to cast powerful spells. However, this primordial magic can be dangerous and can hurt friends and foes alike.

The *ranger* is a wilderness warrior trained in the skills of the wild.
