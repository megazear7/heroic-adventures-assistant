# Character creation

Follow the steps outlined in this section in order to create your character. You will need to print off a character sheet before starting. You will also need a pencil and a d20 dice.

### Choose a Class

Select a class from the below list. Apply the character creation effects described in the class chapter.

The *fighter* takes up weapons and armor and leads his allies into combat. *(+5 health*)

The *wizard* casts spells to control and defeat enemies. *(+3 health*)

The *rogue* likes to stay out of the way of the enemy and take advantage of their weaknesses. *(+3 health*)

The *cleric* is a religious warrior who can also cast healing and support spells. *(+4 health*)

The *priest* is divine intervention to aid his companions in combat. *(+3 health*)

The *paladin* is a divine warrior who is unafraid of even death itself. *(+4 health*)

The *warlock* is spellcaster who uses his own lifeblood as source of magic. *(+3 health*)

The *barbarian* is a warrior who focuses on aggression and power to overcome his enemies. *(+5 health*)

The *druid* uses the forces of nature to cast powerful spells. *(+4 health*)

The *ranger* is a wilderness warrior trained in the skills of the wild. *(+5 health*)

### Choose Features

Select one heroic feature from your class. You may take a feat instead of a class feature.

### Choose Training

Choose two expertise to gain training in and 1 weapon category to gain training in. Fill in the bubbles next to those expertise.

### Choose Equipment

Spend 50 coins to acquire your starting equipment and determine your combat profile.

### Finalize Profile

Your health is determined by your class bonus, race bonus, stat increase selection, willpower, and background.

# Character advancement

After each session, the game master will reward you with experience points. Add up the reward experience points with your current total. When you gain enough total experience points for the next level as indicated in the table below, gain the benefits described.

|                   |                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             |
|-------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Creation          | All the benefits described in the character creation section.                                                                                                                                                                                               |
| Race advancement  | Gain 1 new race feature or feat, race advancement health bonus, and other bonuses described in your race entry. +4 health for dwarf and half orc. +3 health for human, elf, and gnome. +2 health for halfling.                                              |
| Class advancement | Gain 1 new class feature or feat, class advancement health bonus, and other bonuses described in your class entry. +4 health for fighter, paladin, barbarian, and ranger. +3 health for cleric and druid. +2 health for wizard, rogue, priest, and warlock. |
| Training          | Gain training in 2 stats, 2 expertise, 1 weapon category, and increase the number of available heroic actions you have per day by 1. Gain health based upon your background.                                                                                |
| Learning          | Gain training in 2 stats, 2 expertise, 1 weapon category, and gain health equal to your willpower.                                                                                                                                                          |

# <img src="assets/images/media/image6.png" style="width:8.73949in;height:4.36974in" />Feats

Instead of taking race or class features, you may select a feature from the list of feats. These are features that any race or character class can pick from.

### Footwork: Feature

Increase your minor movement speed by 1.

### Swift: Feature

Increase your major movement speed by 1.

### Nimble: Feature

Make a minor movement on the enemy minor action card, before the enemy creatures move.

### Tough: Feature

Increase your health by 4. Requires level 3+.

### Hardy: Feature

Increase your health by 4. Requires Tough and level 9+.

### Learned: Feature

Gain training in 3 different expertise. Reduce the level requirements for all expertise by 3.

### Weapon Training: Feature

Gain training in 2 different weapon categories. Reduce level requirements for all weapon training by 3.

### Ranged Combat Specialist: Feature

When making a ranged attack against a target that is adjacent to a single ally, that ally does not need to make an agility defense roll if you miss your attack.

### Close Range Fighting: Feature

When you make a ranged attack, you may shoot through allies that are not adjacent to the target.

### Dual Wielding: Bonus Action

You may wield two weapons and attack with both weapons when making a bonus attack. Requires level 5+.

### Scholar: Feature

You may spend 1 hour to make a roll on the mages guild table without being at the mage’s guild and modify the result by 1.

### Scroll Master: Feature

You may modify the result of a mage’s guild roll up or down by 3. Requires **Scholar** and intelligence 5+.

### Craftsmen: Feature

When purchasing an artisan or mastercrafted item, spend 1d20 days crafting it instead. The total cost is reduced by 75 for artisan items and 300 for mastercrafted items.

### Quick Draw: Feature

You may switch weapon profiles with a minor action. If you already took a major action this round you cannot do so again even if your new initiative card is drawn.

### Far Shot: Feature

Double the range of all ranged weapons.

### Cavalier: Feature

While mounted, gain +2 initiative and advantage on charge attacks.

### Cook: Feature

Spend 5 coins to purchase from a market and provide a meal for the entire party. Double the healing everyone receives from resting that day.

### Alchemist: Feature

You may spend eather to craft items normally available only from the alchemist's guild. Each eather spent requires 1 hour of work. Requires 5 eather to acquire this feat.

### Valor: Feature

Gain 1 bonus heroic action per day if your background does not already provide one. Requires level 5+.

### Unyielding: Feature

Increase your toughness by 1. Requires level 5+.

### Vampirism: Feature

When you score a critical hit, drain life from a creature you hit with that melee attack: that creature takes an extra 1d6 damage, and you regain health equal to that additional damage dealt. You take double damage from divine spells and take 1d6 damage from sunlight at the start of each turn that you are exposed. Requires 5 scrolls to acquire this feat. Cannot take Lycanthropy.

### Lycanthropy: Feature

Spend a heroic action to transform into a werewolf for the rest of the encounter. Additionally, each time the sun sets, roll a d20. On a roll of a 1 you transform into a werewolf and lose control over your characters actions. While in your werewolf form, you have the stats of a “slayer” monster of your level. Requires 5 scrolls to acquire this feat. Cannot take Vampirism.

### Possessed: Feature

A demon inhabits your body: gain +1 to intelligence and -2 willpower. You must spend 5 eather to acquire this feat. Cannot take Blessed.

### Blessed: Feature

Divine favor guides you: gain +1 to willpower rolls and increase your block by 1 against undead and demons. As a heroic action, allow a character to reroll any dice roll of a 1. Requires 5 eather to acquire this feat. Cannot take Possessed.

### Venom Resistance: Feature

Gain advantage on checks against poison and halve damage from poison effects. Requires 5 ether to acquire this feat.

### Darkvision: Feature

You can see in dim light as if it were bright light and in darkness as if it were dim light, up to 60 spaces. Requires 5 ether to acquire this feat.

### Survivalist: Feature

You regain the normal amount of health when resting in the wild as if you were at an inn or other resting place.

### Double Draft: Feature

You may use two consumables on a minor action or a major action instead of one.

### Cross Training: Feature

You may take a class feature from another class. Requires level 5+ and you must spend 5 eather.

### Multi Class: Feature

You may take a class feature from another class. Requires cross training, level 13+, and you must spend 5 eather.

### Banishment: Feature

Use your willpower for attack and damage against demons. Requires Cleric, Priest, or Paladin.

## Skill

##### Picking Locks (*skill*)

Picking Locks is the art of bypassing mechanical and magical barriers, from rusty dungeon padlocks to enchanted vault doors. Skilled lockpickers use deft fingers and specialized tools—like thieves’ picks or enchanted keys—to disable tumblers or unravel wards. In a world of hidden treasures and monster-guarded secrets, this expertise opens paths to forbidden chambers or ancient crypts. Yet, failure risks triggering traps or alerting foes, making it a gamble of precision and nerve.

##### Picking Pockets (*skill*)

Picking Pockets is the subtle craft of lifting valuables from unsuspecting targets, whether in bustling marketplaces or shadowy tavern corners. It demands agility and stealth, as a deft hand slips into pouches or cloaks to snatch coins, keys, or enchanted trinkets. In a realm of magic, skilled pickpockets might pilfer spell components or cursed artifacts, risking detection by vigilant guards or mystical alarms. This expertise thrives in daring heists or covert missions where wealth and secrets change hands unseen.

##### Setting Traps (*skill*)

Setting Traps is the expertise of crafting snares, pitfalls, and ambushes to ensnare foes or secure lairs. It blends mechanical ingenuity with magical enhancements, from tripwires and pressure plates to enchanted glyphs that freeze or burn intruders. In a world teeming with monsters, traps can guard camps, slow pursuers, or weaken beasts before a fight. This skill demands foresight and cunning, turning the environment into a weapon against the unwary.

##### Sleight of Hand (*skill*)

Sleight of Hand is the mastery of quick, deceptive movements, blending physical dexterity with minor magical illusions. It’s used to palm objects, perform tricks, or conceal small items like daggers or spell scrolls. In a fantasy setting, it might involve vanishing coins, swapping potions, or subtly casting cantrips unnoticed. This expertise shines for rogues, performers, and enchanters who rely on misdirection to outwit foes or dazzle crowds.

##### Fishing (*skill*)

Fishing is the skill of catching aquatic creatures, from mundane fish to magical beasts lurking in enchanted waters. It involves knowledge of bait, tackle, and the habits of elusive prey like merfolk or sea serpents. In a world of adventure, fishing might yield rare ingredients for potions or clues to sunken treasures. This expertise suits patient hunters or seafaring explorers seeking sustenance or secrets from the deep.

##### Healing (*skill*)

Healing is the vital art of mending wounds and curing ailments, blending mundane medicine with magical restoration. Skilled healers use bandages, herbs, and potions alongside spells or divine blessings to close gashes, purge poisons, or revive the fallen. In a perilous realm of swords and monsters, this expertise is a lifeline, sustaining adventurers through brutal battles or cursed afflictions. It’s the difference between life and death, wielded by clerics, medics, and alchemists alike.

##### Tracking (*skill*)

This expertise allows characters to follow trails left by creatures or individuals, identifying footprints, broken branches, and other signs of passage. Skilled trackers can determine the number of beings, their size, and even their speed. This is invaluable for hunting monsters, pursuing fugitives, or avoiding ambushes in the wilderness. In a world of magic and adventure, tracking might also reveal traces of enchanted beasts or hidden lairs.

##### Crafting (*skill*)

Characters with this expertise can create or repair a variety of items, from simple tools to complex magical artifacts. This includes forging swords, brewing potions, or enchanting armor to withstand monster attacks. The quality and effectiveness of the crafted items depend on the character's level of expertise. Such skills are essential for adventurers needing custom gear to face the perils of a fantasy realm.

## Agility

##### Jumping (*agility*)

Jumping is the physical skill of vaulting over obstacles, spanning gaps, or reaching great heights. It’s crucial for crossing treacherous landscapes, fleeing monstrous foes, or accessing hidden perches in ancient ruins. In a fantasy world, magical boots or spells might boost leaps to defy gravity itself. Adventurers rely on this ability to navigate perilous realms with agility and daring.

##### Balancing (*agility*)

Balancing is the knack for staying steady on narrow or shaky surfaces, like tightropes, crumbling ledges, or storm-swept ship decks. It demands sharp agility and focus to avoid falls or resist being toppled by foes. In battle or hazardous terrains—think icy caves or enchanted spans—this skill keeps characters upright and in control. It’s a must for those who tread the most unstable paths.

##### Climbing (*agility*)

Climbing is the expertise of scaling sheer surfaces, from rugged cliffs to the walls of forgotten castles. Experts use ropes, grappling hooks, or even magic like spider climb spells to ascend with confidence. This skill unlocks high treasures, escapes from danger, or vantage points for combat. In a world of towering peaks and lost ruins, climbing paves the way to adventure.

##### Running (*agility*)

Running is the ability to dash quickly on foot, whether pursuing enemies, escaping threats, or racing across vast expanses. It’s key for closing in on foes, outrunning monsters, or covering ground in a hurry. In a fantasy realm, enchanted boots or speed potions might push runners beyond natural limits. This expertise fuels adventurers who thrive on speed and urgency.

##### Stealth (*agility*)

Stealth is the art of moving unseen and unheard, merging with shadows to evade detection. It includes hiding, silent movement, and using surroundings to stay concealed. Magic like invisibility spells or light-bending cloaks can amplify this skill in a fantastical world. It’s indispensable for thieves, scouts, and anyone infiltrating lairs or dodging deadly guardians.

##### Sailing (*agility*)

Sailing is the expertise of commanding watercraft, from tiny boats to majestic ships, across open seas. It requires understanding tides, weather, and sail mechanics to navigate safely. In a fantasy setting, sailors face magical storms, sea beasts, or cursed waters with courage and skill. This ability drives ocean-bound quests, island hunts, and epic naval clashes.

##### Escape Artist (*agility*)

Escape Artist is the expertise of wriggling free from physical restraints—such as ropes, chains, or magical bindings—or squeezing through confined spaces like narrow crevices or barred windows. It relies on exceptional flexibility, precise body control, and quick thinking to contort and maneuver out of entrapment. In a world filled with dungeons, traps, and monstrous captors, this skill is essential for adventurers who find themselves bound, imprisoned, or cornered. Masters of Escape Artist can slip out of combat grapples, break free from captivity, or bypass seemingly impassable obstacles, transforming vulnerability into opportunity with agile cunning.

##### Swimming (*agility*)

Proficiency in swimming allows characters to move efficiently through water, whether crossing rivers, exploring underwater caves, or escaping aquatic monsters. It includes techniques for holding breath, diving, and rescuing others from drowning. This skill is vital for adventures involving sunken treasures or battles against sea-dwelling creatures in a fantasy setting.

## Aim

##### Throwing (*aim*)

This expertise measures your proficiency in accurately propelling objects through the air, whether for combat or practical purposes. Throwing also proves valuable in adventurous tasks requiring accurate projection, such as tossing a rope to an ally across a chasm, hurling a grappling hook to scale a crumbling ruin, or even lobbing a torch into a shadowy lair to reveal hidden dangers. A high Throwing expertise reflects exceptional hand-eye coordination and the ability to judge distances and trajectories effectively, making it a versatile skill.

##### Construction (*aim*)

Construction is the expertise of erecting structures, from basic shelters to formidable stone fortresses. Masters of this craft understand materials, engineering, and tool use to build sturdy defenses or breach enemy walls. In a magical world, construction might weave in enchanted stones for resilience or runes to repel mystical threats. This skill is essential for adventurers seeking to create safe refuges or dismantle obstacles in their path.

##### Artistry (*aim*)

Artistry covers the creation of visual and performing arts, like painting, sculpture, music, and dance. In a magical setting, it can entertain, inspire, or even weave spells through captivating performances. Skilled artists might conjure illusions, sway emotions, or encode secrets in their creations. This expertise shines for bards, enchanters, and heroes who wield creativity to overcome challenges or rally allies.

##### Fletching (*aim*)

The art of making arrows and bolts, ensuring they are balanced and aerodynamic for accurate flight. Expert fletchers can create specialized ammunition, such as fire arrows or poison-tipped bolts, enhancing their effectiveness in combat against monsters. This skill supports archers and crossbow users by providing tailored projectiles for specific threats.

##### Navigation (*aim*)

Using maps, compasses, and celestial bodies, characters with this expertise can determine their location and plot courses through unknown territories. It is vital for sea voyages, desert treks, or forest expeditions where getting lost could mean encountering deadly creatures. In a magical world, navigation might also involve reading enchanted star charts or avoiding cursed lands.

##### Artillery Operation (*aim*)

This expertise involves the use of siege weapons like catapults, ballistae, and trebuchets to target massive monsters or enemy strongholds. Characters skilled in artillery can accurately aim these machines to breach walls, sink ships, or decimate enemy formations from afar. It’s a powerful asset for large-scale battles or defending against giant beasts.

##### Gem Cutting (*aim*)

Gem Cutting expertise represents a character’s mastery in shaping and polishing gems with meticulous accuracy, enhancing their beauty, value, or magical potency. Proficient gem cutters can transform raw stones into dazzling jewels, cut facets to capture light perfectly, or even inscribe tiny runes to imbue the gems with enchantments. In a realm where gems often hold mystical significance—powering artifacts, focusing spells, or serving as currency—this skill is crucial for crafters, enchanters, and treasure hunters alike. It demands a steady hand, keen eye, and deep understanding of crystalline structures, allowing characters to unlock the full potential of these precious resources while showcasing their finesse and attention to detail.

##### Calligraphy (*aim*)

Calligraphy is the art of elegant and precise writing, a skill that blends artistic flair with meticulous control, making it a prized expertise in a world where words can hold both beauty and power. Characters proficient in Calligraphy can create stunning manuscripts, forge official documents with uncanny accuracy, or inscribe runes and symbols that enhance the potency of magical scrolls and artifacts. In a realm where the written word can unlock ancient secrets or bind powerful spells, this expertise is invaluable for scholars, enchanters, and deceivers alike. The steady hand and keen eye required for Calligraphy reflect a character’s exceptional precision, allowing them to craft works of art or magical inscriptions with flawless accuracy, whether they are illuminating a royal decree or etching a rune onto a blade.

## Tactics

##### Leadership (*tactics*)

This expertise enables characters to inspire and command others, boosting morale and coordinating group actions effectively. Whether leading a small adventuring party against a dragon or a large army in a siege, skilled leaders can turn the tide of battle through strategic direction and motivational speeches. It’s key for uniting allies in a monster-filled world.

##### Strategy (*tactics*)

Knowledge of military tactics, formations, and maneuvers allows characters to plan and execute complex battle plans against foes like orc hordes or magical beasts. This includes understanding terrain advantages, flanking maneuvers, and the strengths and weaknesses of different troop types. It’s essential for outsmarting enemies in adventurous conflicts.

##### Diplomacy (*tactics*)

Skilled diplomats can negotiate treaties, resolve conflicts, and persuade others to see their point of view, avoiding bloodshed with cunning words. This expertise is crucial for forming alliances with rival factions or convincing a monster’s minions to stand down. It shines in a fantasy setting rife with political intrigue and magical negotiations.

##### Scouting (*tactics*)

The ability to gather intelligence about enemy positions, numbers, and movements without being detected. Scouts can also assess terrain for strategic advantages or hidden dangers, like monster lairs, providing critical information for planning. This skill is indispensable for staying one step ahead in a perilous adventure.

##### Logistics (*tactics*)

Managing the supply lines, resources, and provisions necessary for sustaining a group over time in long quests or campaigns. This includes rationing food, maintaining equipment, and ensuring the party has what it needs to face magical threats. It’s vital for survival in extended journeys through hostile lands.

##### Siegecraft (tactics)

Expertise in both attacking and defending fortified positions, crucial for battles involving castles or monster-infested ruins. This includes knowledge of siege engines, undermining walls, and strategies for withstanding or breaking sieges. It’s a game-changer in large-scale conflicts or defending against overwhelming odds.

##### Cartography (*tactics*)

Cartography is the art of crafting maps, a vital skill for adventurers exploring uncharted lands teeming with monsters and magic. Skilled cartographers produce detailed maps that reveal hidden trails, mark dangerous lairs, and pinpoint enchanted landmarks. This expertise includes knowledge of geography, political borders, and strategic sites for planning ambushes or defenses. In a realm of swords and sorcery, a precise map can guide a party to triumph or steer them clear of doom.

##### Ambush (*tactics*)

Ambush expertise embodies the art of surprise, deception, and strategic cunning in both combat and exploration. This skill involves planning and executing small-scale operations such as ambushes, feints, and diversions, using the environment to conceal one’s presence or mislead opponents. Characters proficient in Ambush can outmaneuver foes by luring them into traps, striking from hidden vantage points, or coordinating sudden, overwhelming attacks with allies. Whether navigating treacherous dungeons or engaging in guerrilla warfare against a superior force, this expertise is invaluable for adventurers who favor strategy and guile over direct confrontation, turning the element of surprise into a powerful weapon.

## Intelligence

##### Perception (*intelligence*)

This expertise allows characters to notice subtle details, hidden objects, or faint sounds that others might miss. It encompasses keen senses and the ability to interpret sensory information quickly and accurately, making it invaluable for spotting traps, ambushes, or magical auras in the chaos of adventure.

##### Deception (*intelligence*)

Masters of deception can craft convincing lies, disguise their intentions, or create illusions to mislead others. This skill is crucial for spies, tricksters, and mages who weave falsehoods to outwit monsters or rival adventurers.

##### Detecting Lies (*intelligence*)

This expertise grants the ability to read body language, tone of voice, and other tells to determine when someone—or something—is being untruthful. It’s essential for interrogating captured foes, negotiating with cunning merchants, or unmasking disguised magical beings.

##### Finance (*intelligence*)

From raising barns to loading ships, you’ve lent your back to every task needing raw power. No job is too grueling—stone, timber, and grain all bend to your effort. Your hands are calloused, your frame sturdy from years of toil. Work has sculpted you into strength itself.

##### Slave

Forced into relentless labor, you’ve carried burdens and broken rock under a master’s lash. Pain and exhaustion became your teachers, building muscle where hope might have faded. Your body is a testament to survival, forged in chains but never truly bound. Freedom lies in your unyielding strength.

##### Captive

As a captive, you’ve been made to work tirelessly, hauling weight and shattering rock under watchful eyes. Agony and fatigue have been your instructors, instilling resilience where optimism might have waned. Your physique is evidence of your tenacity, molded by shackles yet never completely restrained. True freedom is found in your unwavering strength.

##### Homeless

Scraping by on streets and alleys has made you quick to spot danger or a chance at a meal. You’ve learned to react fast—dodging guards, grabbing scraps, or fleeing trouble. Every day is a gamble, and your sharp instincts keep you alive. The city’s pulse beats in your veins.

##### Refugee

Fleeing war or ruin, you’ve crossed borders with nothing but wits and speed to guide you. You’ve outrun pursuers, hidden from threats, and seized fleeting opportunities to survive. Constant movement has honed your reflexes to a razor’s edge. Readiness is your shield.

##### Scout

Exploring untamed lands, you’ve mapped trails and watched for ambushes with keen senses. Quick feet and quicker decisions have saved you from beasts and bandits alike. The wild demands alertness, and you’ve learned to act before others even see the need. Instinct guides your every step.

##### Messenger

Carrying words across towns and wilds, you’ve dodged obstacles and raced against time. Speed and awareness keep your missives safe from thieves or weather. You know when to run, hide, or push on, reacting in an instant. Urgency is your craft, and you wield it well.

##### Watchman

Guarding walls or streets through dark hours has trained you to catch the slightest hint of trouble. You’ve sounded alarms and drawn steel at a moment’s notice, always ready. Vigilance keeps you one step ahead of danger, your reactions swift and sure. The night sharpens your edge.

In *Heroic Adventures*, every creature is a member of a certain race, such as human, elf, dwarf, goblin, demon, or ogre. Character races, or the races that you can play as a player, are limited to the ones listed in this chapter, and shown in character creation.

Each character race will have certain starting stat modifiers to the primary stats of skill, agility, intelligence, willpower, strength, and initiative. These can be modified during character creation and level advancement. Equipment will also modify the effective statistics that you will use through the course of gameplay.

Depending upon the setting that you are playing in, which is determined by the game master, each race might have different allegiances, feelings, and grudges against other races. It is common for elves and dwarves to not get along, for example, but for halflings to get along with everyone.

<img src="assets/images/media/image13.png" style="width:2.11736in;height:3.51319in" />In addition to your affecting your primary stats, your race will also give you a certain amount of health during character creation and each time you level up.

Your race will also give you features that you can acquire as you level up. These features can give you heroic actions or adjust other aspects of your character.

Some races are tough, like dwarves and half orcs. Other races are quick on their feet, like halflings and elves. Others are balanced, such as humans. Choosing your race is one of the most defining choices you make as you create your character.

Your race will also affect how you roleplay. Some towns or cities might not take kindly to dwarves, for example. This might be due to a war that was fought with the dwarves that is still in the living memory of some of the elders of the community. Or alternatively, elves might be distrusted due to their elusive behavior.

Depending on the world created by the game master and the players, your choice of race will affect gameplay in combat and roleplay out of combat.

An important factor that comes into play during combat is your major and minor movement speed. Major movement speed is how far you can move before or after an attack. Minor movement speed is how far you can move between your major actions. This minor movement speed allows you to get out of attack range at the right time, position yourself in the right place as combat unfolds, and more. Your selection of race will largely determine both attributes.
