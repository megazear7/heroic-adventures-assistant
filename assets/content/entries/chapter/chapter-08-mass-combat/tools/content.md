Mass Combat

At higher levels, characters could fight hordes or armies of enemies. The game master can group creatures that are significantly lower level than the characters into units. When creating a unit, use the statistics of the creature as normal except modify the statistics as shown on the below table.

### Unit Sizes

| Unit size | \# of creatures | Modify statistics | Damage dice |
|-----------|-----------------|-------------------|-------------|
| Warband   | 5               | +2                | +1          |
| Host      | 10              | +3                | +2          |

### Unit Stats

All base statistics, including skill, agility, intelligence, willpower, strength, and initiative are modified by the value from the above table. A units health characteristic is equal to the normal health characteristic of that creature.

### Unit Health Modifications

For creatures that have 12 or more health, it is recommended to reduce the health characteristic by 1 or more and add 1 or more additional creatures to the unit to make up for the lost health. For example, if a lizardman has 16 health, then a warband of 5 lizardmen could have 13 health. This would mean that a total of 15 health was lost by this reduction. To make up for this lost health, one additional lizardmen could be added to the unit, to make up 13 of the 15 lost health. This keeps the total health of the almost the same, but allows for the unit to more easily sustain casualties. It is recommend to make this health modification for any creatures that have 15 or more health, as an attack has to do that much damage just to kill a single creature from the unit.

### Moving Units

A unit that charges into contact with an enemy unit must move straight towards that unit, only turning to avoid obstacles. They must engage that unit on the side that they were facing when they began the charge. Unit formation should be roughly square or rectangular with a clear front line. Any side of the unit that is not the front line is considered the flank.

Once engaged in combat, units cannot make minor movements. When being charged by another unit or an individual character, a unit can turn to face the attacker, if that unit is not already engaged. Individual characters making minor movements while in combat range of a unit must use their minor movement to move straight away from that unit.

### Attacking Units

Units make only a single attack when attacking and roll a single defense roll when being attacked. When being attacked from a side of the unit that is not the front line, the unit gets disadvantage to its agility defense roll. If the unit is not already engaged in melee, then when being charged, a unit can turn to face the charging creature or unit in order to ensure that they charge the front line of the unit.

### Unit Casualties

When taking damage, units track casualties instead of health. Damage reduction is applied as normal. Any attack that does damage equal to or greater than the unit’s health characteristic causes the unit to take a casualty. If an attack doubles the unit’s health characteristic, the unit takes two casualties, and so forth. The health characteristic of a unit is equal to the normal health characteristic for the creature.

### Bravery Checks

After taking casualties, if over half the creatures in a unit have been killed, that unit must make a bravery check. Make a willpower vs. willpower roll against the attacker. If the unit loses this check, they flee the battlefield, making an immediate full move away from the attacker, causing opportunity attacks. Fleeing units continue to flee the battlefield each time their initiative comes up, unless they are rallied.

### Rally

Any character or individual leader amongst an army may attempt to rally a fleeing unit by moving adjacent to the unit and spending their major action rallying the unit. A character adjacent to a unit that is about to make a bravery check can spend his major action in order to give that unit advantage to their rally check.

### Area of Effect

Any attacks that do area of affect damage hit units as a single attack roll. Gain one damage dice for each model within the unit that is covered by the area of affect.

### Army Composition

These mass combat rules can be used to allow a group of characters to fight a dozen or two goblins but can also be used to have two armies clash in large scale combat of up to a hundred or more creatures.

It is recommended to have one individual creature act as a leader, such as an orc chief, for every three units. The player characters can act as leaders for one side of the battle and can control the movement of the units on that side. The players could decide who amongst them will control which units, while the game master controls all the units and creatures on the other side of the battlefield. It can also be fun to mix in ranged units, melee units, individual leader creatures, and individual monsters such as ogres, trolls, and giants.

### Unit Features

Units can also be given additional features that cause them to play differently than other units. The same unit can have multiple features.

#### Skirmisher

Cannot be flanked

#### Shield Wall

+1 block

#### Veteran

+1 skill and agility

#### Rabble

-1 skill and agility

#### Brave

Gain advantage on bravery checks

#### Fearless

Automatically pass bravery checks

#### Fearful

Gain disadvantage on bravery checks

#### Cavalry

When charging, gain advantage on the attack and gain an extra damage dice. Cavalry units may leave combat without drawing opportunity attacks. When being attacked by units with a polearm gain disadvantage to the defense agility roll. This requires the unit be mounted on some form of cavalry such as horses (for humans) or something similar such as spiders (for goblins).

## Units

The below are some examples of units that could be created.

#### Orc Warband

5 creatures, +2 to all stats, +1 damage dice

#### Goblin Warband (Fearful)

5 unit strength, +2 to all stats, +1 damage dice, disadvantage on bravery checks

#### Human Warband (Shield Wall)

5 creatures, +2 all stats, +1 block

#### Krimkar Host (Brave, Veteran, Skirmisher)

10 creatures, +3 all stats, +1 skill, agility, +2 damage dice, advantage on bravery checks, cannot be flanked

#### Elven Host (Veteran, Brave)

10 creatures, +3 all stats, +1 skill and agility, +2 damage dice, advantage on bravery checks

#### Dwarven Host (Fearless)

10 creatures, +3 all stats, +2 damage dice, automatically passes bravery checks
