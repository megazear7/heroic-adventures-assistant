Proteges

As you adventure, the chance of a character dying increases. Death could come from anywhere; dragons, armies, undead lords, or a simple goblin spear that happens to be at the right place at the wrong time. When a character dies, you will of course need to create a new character. However, if you start from level 1 and everyone else is at higher level, then you will find it hard to contribute to the adventure. Protégé’s are designed to solve this problem.

Every great hero, and not so great hero, has someone that he has taken under his wing. Someone to confide in, someone he can trust. This other character learns and grows along with the hero and is always looking for his moment to shine. A time to step up and take his place in the world.

It's just unfortunate that the bad luck of the hero is the long-awaited moment to shine of the protégé, but sometimes that is how it goes.

When you reach level 3 you may create a new character to be your protégé. Your protégé increases in level any time your primary character increases in level. If your primary character dies, you may play your protégé character with all the same in-world knowledge of your primary character. It is recommended to work with the game master to determine your primary character’s relationship to your protégé, why they know each other, and why they share information with each other.

If your protégé becomes your primary character and he is level 3, you may create a new protégé. If your new primary character is higher than level 3, make a protégé character and increase their level such that they are two levels lower than your new primary character.

In this way, you will always have a character waiting in the wing. From a game mechanics perspective this keeps everyone at similar level. It also grows the anticipation to try out a new character, and a character that can quickly be introduced in the middle of a gaming session.

From a roleplay perspective, protégé’s provide an opportunity to expand the game world and provide the main characters relationships with other characters. Where do the characters go when they are not adventuring, who do they spend time with, and where do they live? The protégé characters might be involved in answering all of these questions.

Also, if you want a break from the main story, everyone can play their protégé character for a session. This can be a side quest to get the players used to their new characters and offer a change up to the normal game session.
