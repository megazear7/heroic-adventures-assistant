Monsters

Monsters are an essential part of Heroic Adventures, providing challenges, adversaries, and opportunities for adventurers. Monsters come in a wide variety of forms, from legendary creatures to common foes, and serve different purposes in the game world.

**Adversaries**: Monsters can serve as adversaries for the player characters, providing challenges and obstacles to overcome. They can be powerful creatures, such as dragons or giants, or smaller foes, such as goblins or skeletons. These encounters can test the players combat skills, strategies, and resource management, adding excitement and tension to the gameplay.

**Environmental Hazards**: Some monsters are not necessarily adversaries in the traditional sense, but rather environmental hazards or obstacles. For example, a treacherous swamp might be home to giant crocodiles or poisonous snakes, or a dense forest might be inhabited by hostile creatures like trolls or sproutlings. These monsters can add depth and complexity to the game world, forcing the players to navigate dangerous terrain and make strategic decisions to avoid or defeat them.

**Role-playing Opportunities**: Monsters can offer rich role-playing opportunities for players, allowing them to interact with unique and memorable characters. For example, a mind slayer or an ocean lord might provide opportunities for diplomacy, negotiation, or creative problem-solving, challenging the players to think beyond combat and use their wits or social skills to overcome obstacles.

**Loot and Rewards**: Monsters often guard valuable treasure, magical items, or rare resources that the players can obtain by defeating them. This can provide a strong incentive for adventurers to seek out and defeat powerful monsters and adds an element of risk versus reward to the gameplay. Monsters can also drop valuable components, such as ether and scroll fragments which can be used for crafting.

**Monster sizes**: Most monsters are roughly the size of a human and take up 1 space on the game board. Other monsters are much bigger, such as ogres, giants, and dragons, and can take up 2 spaces, a 2x2 grid of spaces, or more. It is up to the game master to decide how much space a monster takes up on the board, which is largely dependent on what miniatures you have available.

**Homebrew and Creativity**: One of the joys of tabletop roleplaying is the ability to create your own monsters, either by modifying existing ones or inventing entirely new creatures. This allows the game master and players to exercise their creativity and tailor the game world to their own liking. Homebrew monsters can be used to create unique challenges, puzzles, or encounters that reflect the gamemasters or players imagination and storytelling style.

**Spell casting**: Some monsters have access to mana and spells and will be listed in the monster’s feature list. In this case, monsters can cast spells following the normal rules and can apply one enhancement per monster level.

**Monster strength**: When needing to know a monster’s strength, refer to the static bonus to in their damage stat, ignoring and indicated dice. So, if a monster’s damage is 2d6+5, then the monster’s strength is 5.

## Monster profiles

Monster profiles can be used to quickly determine the stats for a wide range of unique monsters that fit your adventure. Each level range has statistics for each of the monster profiles listed here. This means for low levels, you might use the soldier profile for goblins, the leader profile for the goblin chief, the brute profile for orcs, and the behemoth profile for ogres. At higher levels you might use the minion profile for the same orcs, use the brute profile for ogres, and the leader profile for the ogre chieftain.

### Minion

Minions are weak and fragile creatures that follow the orders of others. They are often used to soak up the damage of opponents and often their only strategy is to use the sheer force of numbers to defeat their enemies.

Examples of minions for low level characters is untrained goblins, spiders, and rats. At higher levels minions could represent orc soldiers or demonic hordes.

### Soldier

Soldiers are creatures trained for war. They fight as a unit, use advanced tactics, and come to the fight prepared with weapons and armor.

Examples of soldiers are trained goblins and orc warriors and town guards.

### Beast

Beasts are creatures that are tough and dangerous, but have low intelligence, do not communicate with other creatures or use tactics, and are generally set lose against enemy combatants but not easily controlled.

Examples of beasts are wolves, giant spiders, alligators, and lions.

### Brute

Brutes are tough and do a lot of damage. They are usually few in number and coordinate with soldiers under the direction of leaders to act as the hammer blow against their enemies.

Examples of brutes at low levels could be orcs and demons. At high levels brutes could be ogres and trolls.

### Behemoth

Behemoths are large creatures that are very tough and do devastating damage. Often there is only or two behemoths in a combat.

At low levels behemoths are things like ogres and trolls. At high levels a behemoth could be a gargantuan spider, drake, or giants.

### Slayer

Slayers are highly skilled and do high damage, but in general are less armored than brutes and soldiers. They are used to stand toe to toe with the best of the enemy combatants.

Examples of slayers are assassins, skilled warriors and ruthless barbarians.

### Commander

Commanders have high stats, provide direction to their allies, and control enemy combatants in various ways in order to manipulate the combat to their advantage.

Examples of leaders are chieftains and captains.

### Leader

Leaders have average stats and are generally vulnerable in combat but often have access to spells and abilities to damage the enemy and help their allies.

Examples of mages are orc shamans and human wizards.

## Monster features

Monsters will have various features similar to player characters. These features mean that not all level 4 brutes are the same, for example. Each monster feature will have a recommend level range. This level range is only a recommendation, and more powerful abilities can be given to monsters outside of the recommended range. If a monster has a heroic action but not the “Heroic (x)” feature, then it is assumed that they have access to a single use of that heroic action.

# Mage Spells

## Damage (*minor*)

Choose a creature within 8 spaces and do 1d4 damage that ignores damage reduction

Enhance: Add 1d4 damage

Enhance: Double the range

## Major Damage (*major*)

Choose a creature within 8 spaces and make an arcana vs. willpower attack that does 1d6 + arcana damage that ignores damage reduction.

Enhance: Add 1d6 damage

Enhance: Double the range

## Area Damage (*major*)

Chose a space within 8 spaces. Make an arcana vs. willpower attack against all enemy creatures within 1 space that does 1d6 + arcana damage.

Enhance: Double the distance of the effect from the chosen space

## Control (*minor*)

Choose a space within 8 spaces to place a marker. Enemies that make any move into or out of any space within 2 spaces of the marker have all movements reduced by 1.

## Empower (*minor*)

Choose a space within 8 spaces to place a marker. Allies within 2 spaces of the marker gain advantage to attacks and defense.

# Diviner Spells

## Heal (*minor*)

Heal a target creature within 8 spaces for 1d12 health.

Enhance: Add 1d12 to the healing.

## Protect (*minor*)

Give all allies within 4 spaces advantage to agility and willpower defense rolls.

## Hex (*minor*)

Choose a target enemy creature. All attacks made against that creature until the end of the next round gain bonus damage equal to this the diviners willpower.

## Ward (*minor*)

The next ally within 4 spaces to be damaged by a spell ignores half the damage.
