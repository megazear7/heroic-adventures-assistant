# <img src="assets/images/media/image38.png" style="width:8.98542in;height:4.49236in" />Armor

In *Heroic Adventures*, armor is critical to staying alive. While it slows you down, as represented by the decrease in agility, it also gives you toughness, which is subtracted from most damage that you take. While it might seem like the penalty for wearing armor is high, in reality that increased toughness means that you are preserving your health every time that you get hit, and this is a massive benefit.

In rare cases it might make sense to maximize your agility so that you can avoid getting hit, and risk that when you get hit you will take a lot of damage. This can be done effectively, but it is risky, and it means that one bad role could be very dangerous for your character. Normally, going into battle without armor is a bad idea. A rogue that likes to sneak around and avoid being targeted might be a character that benefits from not wearing armor.

In some cases, however, armor can be less than helpful. If you are fighting a giant that is swinging a tree trunk as a weapon, your toughness might not be helpful. Subtracting 6 damage from a massive attack that does 4d6 damage is not going to help. If the giant hits you, no amount of armor can save you. In this case, it might be worth shedding the armor and avoid getting hit at all costs.

In other circumstances, such as when you are fighting against small weapons from many opponents, armor can make you nearly invincible to their attacks. Every hit they do might barely do enough damage, if any, to penetrate your damage reduction. When this happens, your opponents are going to have to look for ways to get around your armor. This can be done with attacks that ignore armor such as fire attacks, mental attacks, spells, and so forth.

# Shields

In *Heroic Adventures*, shields are the first line of defense. When rolling a defense roll, a shield provides a static chance that you block the opponent’s attack, regardless of the skill of the attacker. Best of all, this happens when you roll low on your agility roll, meaning that when you are most likely to be hit by an attack, you block the attack instead. See the combat section in the gameplay chapter for more information regarding attack and defense rolls.

Each shield will have a size of small, medium, or large, which could be referenced by various game effects. In addition, shields modify your initiative, meaning that you are slightly less quick on your feet than you would otherwise be. However, this comes with the benefit of providing a block value, meaning that when you roll equal to or lower than the listed block value, the attack misses completely. Block values are only used when you are rolling an agility defense roll.

Some attacks might mention that they ignore shields, in which case you would not get this benefit. Examples of these kinds of attacks are area effects, some heroic actions, and large attacks such as a stomp of a giant. In these circumstances, especially the last example, a shield is clearly not going to help!

## Shield grades

The negative initiative modifier of artisan shields is reduced by one, and so an artisan small shield reduced initiative by one instead of two and an artisan large shield reduced initiative by three instead of four. The negative initiative modifier of mastercrafted shields is reduced by two, and so a mastercrafted small shield does not reduce initiative and a mastercrafted medium shield reduces initiative by one instead of three.

## Magic shields

Some shields are considered magical. Magical shields have an associated enchantment, and a number indicating how powerful the enchantment is, such as +7 medium shield divinity. You may use the indicated ability after successfully blocking with your shield. # <img src="assets/images/media/image48.png" style="width:0.57596in;height:2.22065in" />Weapons

In *Heroic Adventures*, the most common action is the weapon attack. Weapon attacks can be performed during a major action, before or after movement. Other actions will indicate if and when you can make a weapon attack, as detailed in the action description.

<img src="assets/images/media/image49.png" style="width:0.48333in;height:2.61319in" />Each weapon has a certain cost for the standard, artisan, and master crafted versions of the weapon. Some weapons are one handed, which means that they can be equipped simultaneously with a second weapon, shield, other object, or an open hand. Additionally, weapons will have a size of small, medium, or large. Various game effects may reference the size of the weapon.

Weapons have a range, such as 1 space, 2 spaces, or more. Most melee weapons have a range of 1 space, some will have a longer range. Ranged weapons such as bows will have a much longer range.

Additionally, weapons will modify your base initiative. This means that fast weapons will have a higher initiative and will be more likely to get bonus attacks each round. Slow weapons will likely do more damage but will lower your initiative meaning you are less likely to get bonus attacks. Lastly, each weapon will have a damage dice characteristic which tells you what dice to roll when you hit with an attack.

# Alchemists Guild

Alchemists do not normally use standard coins for currency. Instead, they trade in ether, which they use to perfect their craft and produce potent potions and items that even the most powerful are envious of. Their secret craft is sought to be controlled by all who desire power. Yet, the cunning alchemist guild keeps tight rein on their secrets through their use of archaic languages and encrypted symbolism.

The guild even foes so far as to prevent the sale of potions from anyone other than proper members of the alchemist guild. Kings will help to enforce this rule, as to not lost access to the benefits that the alchemists guild brings to society.

Alchemy items are consumables items purchased with earther. While they will also accept payment with coins for some potions, it is advisable to use ether for purchasing potions while saving coins for higher tier equipment such as artisan and mastercrafted weapons and armor.

Potions can be forcibly given with agility vs. agility or strength vs. strength potion.

## Health potion (1 ether or 50 coins)

Minor action: Heal 1d12 health. The minimum heal is equal to your willpower.

## Energy potion (1 ether or 50 coins)

Minor action: In the next round, act on the highest initiative in the encounter deck and may take a minor movement on the enemy’s minor action initiative card, before any enemies move.

## Strength potion (1 ether or 50 coins)

Minor action: Gain an extra weapon dice during your next melee weapon attack during this encounter

## Bravery potion (1 ether or 50 coins)

Minor action: Take a heroic action.

## Wardstone (1 ether or 100 coins)

Reaction: After being damaged by a spell, you may use this wardstone to reduce the damage by half.

## Smoke bomb (1 ether or 50 coins)

Minor action: Throw the smoke bomb to a space within 6 spaces, covering all spaces within 2 of the target in smoke. Creatures cannot make ranged attacks into or out of the smoke or make attacks on turns in which they moved into or out of the smoke or take opportunity actions while within the smoke.

## Spell potion (1 ether or 50 coins)

Reaction: Add one enhancement to a spell that you are currently casting. You may use up to three spell potions at a time.

## Sleeping potion (3 ether or 200 coins)

Minor action: A creature is put to sleep for 4d6 hours.

## Stone of Transmutation (5 ether)

Minor action: Move or swap spell enchantments associated between two items.

# Mages Guild

The mages guild is a loose association of the magically adept that nevertheless wield and retain tremendous influence over the mortal races. Their magic abilities are always sought after whether for war, negotiation, or assassination. The mages guild is always looking to grow in knowledge and learning and so will readily interpret magic scrolls and artifacts. Their scribes will copy the symbols, ancient languages, and mysterious ciphers into their library, while providing their customers with the ability to use the scroll on their own.

## Scrolls

While adventuring, players will collect fragments that they can bring to a mages tower or guild house. Each interpreted fragment gives the player a roll on the below table to see what level 1 spell, if any, the fragment provides as a single use scroll. If multiple spells are listed for a single result, you may choose the spell, but the choice must be made as soon as the fragment is interpreted. Multiple fragments can be combined into a single roll. For each fragment combined, the level of the scroll’s spell increases by 1. You can only spend a number of scroll fragments equal to your intelligence. You can also spend additional fragments after the roll in order to modify the result by 1 for each fragment you spend.

## Torches

Torches require one free hand and provide light within 10 spaces of the wielder. They require a major action to light. A character can carry up to five torches in their inventory.

## War Horse

Mounted movement: 6 / 6
