# Rule: GM Worldbuilding and NPCs

## Scope

Use this rule for setting construction, NPC motivation design, repeat locations, and campaign continuity management.

## Consolidated Rule

GM prep emphasizes dynamic settings over rigid scripts. Build places and motivated NPCs, then let player choices drive sequence. Encounters and consequences should react to player action, not force a prewritten plot.

## Setting Procedure

1. Define locations with actionable detail.
2. Define NPC motivations and conflicting goals.
3. Prepare encounter packets tied to those goals.
4. Place clues/hooks that support multiple player routes.
5. Reconcile world state after sessions.

## NPC and Protégé Integration

- Recurring NPCs strengthen continuity and player investment.
- NPCs can become protégés when relationship criteria are met.
- Motivations should evolve based on player impact.

## Campaign Pressure Tools

- Resource pressure between major scenes.
- Escalating consequences when threats are ignored.
- Repeat locations with changing stakes.

## Adjudication Notes

- Prefer “world reacts” rulings over “plot protects itself.”
- Keep explicit clocks/tensions to drive credible NPC behavior.
