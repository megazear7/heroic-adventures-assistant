# Rule: Spells and Casting Systems

## Scope

Use this rule for all spellcasting adjudication across player spell lists and monster spell templates.

## Consolidated Rule

Spell systems include mystic, rune, nature, and divine spell lists for characters, plus mage/diviner templates for monsters. Each spell has an action type (minor or major), target logic, and effect text.

## Casting Timing

- Minor spells resolve in normal minor-action timing.
- Major spells are prepared and resolve on all-players/all-monsters card as applicable.
- Prepared major casters suffer disadvantage to defense while holding spell.
- Taking damage while preparing can cause self-inflicted disruption damage per rule text.

## Spell Resolution Procedure

1. Verify legal action window and sufficient mana.
2. Declare target/area and enhancements.
3. Resolve attack vs defense where required.
4. Apply damage/healing/control effects.
5. Spend mana at proper timing point.

## Defensive Interaction

- Ward effects, resistance effects, and shield/cover logic only apply where text permits.
- Some spell effects ignore armor or damage reduction explicitly.

## Monster Casting Integration

- Monsters with Mage(x) or Diviner(x) gain mana and access to spell templates.
- Monster enhancement budget scales with monster level per monster rules.

## Adjudication Notes

- Keep spell timing strict; timing is core balance lever.
- If two effects conflict, apply explicit spell text over generic assumptions.
