# Rule: Encounter Building and Difficulty

## Scope

Use this rule for building easy/standard/difficult encounters, estimating pressure, and tuning encounter flow.

## Consolidated Rule

Encounter difficulty is based on relative total level of monsters vs participating characters. Difficulty should map to expected resource drain and tactical stress.

## Difficulty Bands

- Easy: monsters far below party total level pressure.
- Standard: monsters around a meaningful but manageable fraction/equivalence to party total level.
- Difficult: monsters above party total level and potentially up to roughly double.

## Encounter Design Procedure

1. Define objective and battlefield context.
2. Choose monster role composition.
3. Validate level pressure against target difficulty.
4. Add escalation options (reinforcements, terrain shifts, objective changes).
5. Assign reward profile consistent with risk.

## Pacing Principles

- Diversify threats (willpower pressure, high initiative, armor bypass, control effects).
- Use encounter flow to chain pressure across scenes.
- Include retreat-worthy scenarios to preserve stakes.

## Adjudication Notes

- Tune during play by reinforcement timing and objective pressure rather than hidden stat inflation.
- Track consumables/mana/heroic usage as primary pressure indicators.
