# Loot and Rewards

## Purpose

Generate rewards that feel meaningful, fit encounter difficulty, and maintain healthy campaign progression.

## When to Use

- After encounters, quests, or milestones.
- During session prep to prebuild reward tables.
- When calibrating economy and item power growth.

## Required Inputs

- Party level and size.
- Recent reward history (to avoid repetition and inflation).
- Encounter/adventure difficulty and risk profile.
- Campaign economy norms and scarcity assumptions.

## Workflow

1. Set reward intent: recovery aid, power bump, narrative leverage, or long-term progression.
2. Allocate reward mix: currency/ether, consumables, gear opportunities, and non-material rewards.
3. Scale reward value to difficulty and party status.
4. Add narrative linkage (who grants it, why, and consequences).
5. Include at least one flexible reward type usable by multiple party members.
6. Add economy guardrails to prevent runaway accumulation.

## Quality Checks

- Reward impact matches challenge faced.
- No single drop invalidates class identity or encounter pressure.
- Consumable flow supports attrition gameplay.
- Narrative rewards have actionable follow-through.

## Output Template

Return:
- Reward package with category breakdown.
- Short justification for balance and pacing.
- Immediate usability notes.
- Optional alternatives (conservative vs generous).
- Power-spike warning notes if any risk exists.

## Missing Information Policy

If economy context is unknown, default to conservative rewards, state assumptions, and provide one upgrade option the GM can add if the table is underpowered.

## Rule Routing

- Anchor recommendations to consumables, loot/economy, equipment, and encounter difficulty rule skills.
- If pricing or rarity guidance is unclear, provide safe ranges and state uncertainty.
